Here are a few of my favorite books.

Go ahead, click on a book to learn more.

Endurance: Shackleton's Incredible Voyage by Alfred Lansing. This book tells the tale of the sailing ship Endurance and Captain Ernest Shackleton and crew on their expedition to the South pole. Their journey only begins when the Endurance gets locked in ice forcing the Captain and crew to find their way out of a dicy situation on the barren ice pack.

The Millionaire Next Door: The Surprising Secrets of America's Wealthy by Thomas Stanley and William Danko. Everyone has a perception in their head of what a millionaire should look like. Dr. Thomas Stanley and William Danko are hear to crush those misperceptions and lay down the facts of how millionaires live, act, and work their way to the top.

Financial Peace Revisited by Dave Ramsey. Someone who has been bankrupt once, yet a millionaire twice in his life thus far surely must have some money advice to give. Dave Ramsey does just that in his easy to read book, Financial Peace Revisted. This no-nonsense book lays the groundwork for a financially illiterate reader to work their way through debt to success.

Flags of Our Fathers by James Bradley. The author's father, John Bradley, is one the men raising up the American flag on Iwo Jima in World War II. John Bradley goes into great detail of the story of how his father and the other five men got to raising that flag. Their background, the war, and the outcome that raising that flag had on each of them. Even if you have no prior interest in military or war, this book is sure to hold your attention.

Steve Jobs by Walter Isaacson. Many know that Steve Jobs was a complicated man. To get some context on this statement, be sure to read this book. Learn about Steve's childhood and his background in electronics, all the way through to his achievement at Apple Computers and how he has changed the world forever. Steve left nothing off the table about his life for Walter Isaacon to write about; his triumphs, failures, and his own personal demons.

Big Coal: The Dirty Secret Behind America's Energy Future by Jeff Goodell. I don't consider myself a tree hugger and I still got plenty of enjoyment out of this book. Learn about where half the electricity in the United States comes from. Learn about where coal comes from, how it is used, and the aftermath of using it. But should we be giving up on coal if it is such a low-cost energy source and there is a plethora of it available? Read Big Coal to form your own opinion.
